What is "Detailing"?

Detailing is truly an art that a detailer must have a craving passion for, in order for them to be the best possible. This is why it is extremely important to find a detailer that you know is informed and reliable. Detailing consists of the deep cleaning, decontamination from any films or embeded particles, and the protection of the vehicle as well as dressing to make the car look more appealing and clean.

For most vehicles, there are 2 main areas that a person may be looking to get detailed which would be the Exterior and the Interior. Typically, you will find services for the exterior such as cleaning the paint as well as the wheels and tires. Paint decontamination is one of the most important steps in an exterior detail, because it prepares the vehicle for the next step which is protection. Typically, you will find 2 types of protection when getting your car detailed which are sealants (formerly known as waxes) and coatings. Sealants are more of a shorter term protection for the paint whereas coatings provide a longer protection window, but are typically more costly. As far as the interior goes, there are specific things that you should look for when getting your interior detailed, however there are a few extra services that are available if you desire. The most common and important extra service that is typically available is carpet and upholsterly extraction. This is used for more deep cleans, however it can be quite costly.

However, the most important aspect of getting your car detailed is who is detailing your car. Every detailer does things a little different and offer different services with different packages, so it is extremely important to have an open conversation with anyone before they detail your car about what they are providing and the results that you should expect. At the end of your reading on this website, you should be able to be informed of the most typical services that are available at many local detailing shops, and know what those services will actually do for you and your vehicle.
