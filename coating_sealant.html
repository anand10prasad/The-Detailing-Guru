Coatings and Sealants

Coatings and Sealants are the most common types of paint protection, with others including traditional waxes and Paint Protection Films (PPF). However, traditional waxes are both more time consuming to apply and less effective as well as less longevity, when compared to the more modern sealants. Paint Protection Films are extremely expensive which is why many detail shops do not offer the service. I will focus on Coatings and Sealants as they are the most common and price efficient options that are available at most detail shops. One of the most common misconceptions about paint protection is that the paint protection will protect your vehicle for at least the amount of time specified. However this is not true, as coatings and sealants are rated for a maximum amount of longevity if they are properly taken care of and maintained. So if you have a 2 year coating or a 6 month sealant, in order for it to last that long, you must maintain it as per your detailers reccomendations. The last and most important factor as to why a person would get protection applied on their car is for the ease of maintenance. Coatings and Sealants make it 10x easier to remove any dirt on the surface of the vehicle which will make it easier to clean the next time your vehicle gets dirty.

Paint Sealants

Paint sealants are the most common type of paint correction. They are closely related to the traditional waxes that we all know of that take a longer amount of time to apply and remove, as well as don't have the same longevity as sealants. Sealants are a more modern version of the traditional waxes that we are used to. Typically a wax will only last roughly a month on your vehicles paint, however a paint sealant can last many multiple months with some new companies introducing up to a 12 month sealant. Getting a sealant put on is a great idea if you get your car detailed often or you dont get paint correction done on the vehicle.

Paint Coatings

Paint Coatings are a more long term protection. Top of the line coatings can range anywhere from 1 year of protection to 10 years of protection if properly maintained. The highlight of coatings over sealants is the hardness and the ease of maintanance. Most professional coatings are anywhere from a 9-10 on the pencil hardness scale. Not only that, but these coatings are extremely hydrophobic as well as oliophobic which means they reject water and oils very well. This means that cleaning the vehicle after it has been coated is extremely easy compared to before the coating. It is highly reccomended that you apply a coating after a paint correction in order to ensure the paints integrity and saftey from the outside world. However there are some instances where you may want to apply a coating on a brand new vehicle, however check with your detailer if this is the case as many new vehicles with 0 miles have small scratches in the paint from the manufacturer which can be polished out easilly before a permanent coating is applied.
